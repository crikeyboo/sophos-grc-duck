# Coverage Definitions

This document defines the approved coverage categories for mapping Sophos product and service capabilities to cybersecurity frameworks.

## Direct

The Sophos capability directly supports the control outcome with clear evidence from product documentation, technical architecture, configuration guidance, service description, detection content, API specification, or product owner confirmation.

Direct coverage does not mean the customer is compliant. It means the capability can directly support implementation or operation of the control when deployed and configured appropriately.

## Partial

The Sophos capability supports part of the control outcome but does not satisfy the full intent of the control.

Partial coverage should identify which aspect of the control is supported and which aspects remain outside the capability.

## Enabling

The Sophos capability provides telemetry, visibility, workflow, automation, reporting, alerting, evidence, or operational support that helps the customer meet the control.

Enabling coverage is common where a product supports monitoring, investigation, reporting, assurance, or evidence collection, but does not itself implement the full control.

## Compensating

The Sophos capability may reduce risk where the primary expected control is absent, incomplete, or implemented differently.

Compensating coverage must be used cautiously and should include a clear caveat explaining what risk is reduced and what risk remains.

## Requires Customer Configuration

The Sophos capability can support the control only if the customer enables, configures, scopes, tunes, integrates, or operates it correctly.

This category should be used where the product provides the functionality, but the control outcome depends materially on customer configuration.

## Requires Service or Process

The Sophos capability is insufficient on its own and requires a customer process, managed service, incident response process, governance activity, policy, operating procedure, or human review workflow.

This category is important where the control requires accountability, decision-making, escalation, review, testing, or evidence retention.

## Not Covered

No meaningful Sophos capability has been identified that supports the control outcome.

Not Covered should be used where the product or service does not address the control, or where available evidence is insufficient to support a mapping.

## Prohibited Claims

Mappings must not state or imply that Sophos products make a customer compliant with ISO 27001, CIS Controls, NIST CSF, NCSC CAF, SCF, DORA, NIS2, Cyber Essentials, or any other framework or legal requirement.

Approved language:

“Sophos supports implementation and evidence of selected control outcomes where deployed, configured and operated within an appropriate governance framework.”

Avoid language such as:

“Sophos makes customers compliant.”
“Sophos satisfies this control.”
“This product guarantees compliance.”
